About Me

My name is Matthew Boensch, and I am a senior at the University of Oklahome, attending Price College of Business, where I am amjoring in MIS, Marketing, and Management. During my time at Price, I have maintained a 3.94 GPA and have developed many relationships with my professors and my mentors.

Early Life

As a child, I was born and raised in New Jersey. As a kid, I always found an interest in technology and knew that I wanted to work in that field. When I was 13, I moved to Texas, where I began taking more of an interest in marketing. This interestest was furthered by joinging my high school's DECA chapter. DECA is an organization that encourages innovation, problem solving, and professionalism in a business setting. In DECA, we would go to tournaments where we would be provided with a case about a fictional business, some problems that they are facing, some background, and we would have 10 min to prepare a solution and present it to the "boss" in our "meeting." DECA utilized roleplaying scenarios to help students begin developing their professional and soft skills at an early age. During my 2 years in DECA, I attended 2 statewide competitions as well as 1 national compeition. This is where my interest in marketing really started to become aparent to me.

University Life

I began my Freshman year at the Univeristy of Oklahoma in the Fall of 2017. When I first arrived at OU, I decided that my major was to be marketing, as DECA helped me realize that this was something I was interested in. I'm not sure what clicked in my brain during my Freshman year, but all of the sudden I became very motivated to achieve and do well in my classes. I was never a slacker by any means, however, during high school I was happy getting all B's in my classes. I don't know what happened, but whenever I got to collefe I suddenly had a lot more motivation and dicipline than before. I strived to be the top of my class and make sure that my college education was the best it could be. Along the road, I did hit a few bumbps in the road and recived a B in 2 classes, but besides those outliers I have been successful in reciveing an A in almost all of my courses.

While at OU, I speant a lot of time going to football games, as I have always loved the sport (I played for a few years in high school before I got a bad injury) and I also got involved in campus life. I joined the Housing Center Student Association (HCSA) where I was elected the Director of Spirit and Involvement. My job was to lead the HCSA executive board and HCSA members in our participation with many campus events, like Homecoming and Big Event (OU's day of volunteering and service.) During my time as the Director of S&I, I wanted to do more for the HCSA members, so I created a spirit group aimed at Freshmen on campus who wanted to attend events or do something on the weekdays but didn't have anyone to go to the events with. When I was a Freshman, my roomate rarely left our dorm, and a lot of my friends were not big into sports and would typically leave during half time (if they went at all.) This always annoyed me, so I wanted to help others not have to experiece the same issues I had

During my time at OU, I also worked as a residential hall aid. This job involved me sitting at a desk in 2 of the 4 Freshman Residence halls and answering any questions the students had. This job not only helped me practice my hospitality and customer service skills, but it also allowed me to meet many Freshman and develop relationships with those who frequented the ground level of the residence halls. The ground level had a community kitchen, pool table, ping-pong table, and a bunch of comfy chairs to hang out with friends. I was able to get to know many of the residents, and even convinced a few of them to join HCSA.
